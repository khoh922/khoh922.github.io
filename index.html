About

I am a Moore Foundation Postdoctoral Fellow in Physical Chemistry at the UC Berkeley, working with Prof. Michael Zuerch. I completed my PhD in Physics at MIT under the supervision of Prof. Nuh Gedik, where I studied the light-induced dynamics of coexisting charge density wave systems using time-resolved X-ray and electron diffraction techniques.

Before joining MIT, I served as a commissioned officer in the Republic of Korea Air Force (ROKAF) during my mandatory military service (2017–2020), conducting research at the Agency for Defense Development (ADD). During my undergraduate studies at POSTECH, I worked in Prof. K. S. Kim’s group (now at Yonsei University), where I investigated oxygen-induced degradation of black phosphorus using synchrotron photoemission spectroscopy (2015–2017). In Fall 2015, I also joined Prof. Andreas Wallraff’s quantum device group at ETH Zurich as an exchange student researcher.

My research focuses on probing the fundamental interactions between light and quantum materials, as well as stabilizing nonequilibrium phases of matter through ultrafast optical excitation. I study light-induced dynamics in quantum systems including charge density waves, superconductors, and magnetic materials using time-resolved diffraction and spectroscopy techniques spanning temporal regimes from picoseconds (10⁻¹² s) to attoseconds (10⁻¹⁸ s).
